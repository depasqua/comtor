Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who is COMTOR for?

COMTOR is provided as a free service in the hopes that it will benefit the educational community. COMTOR is provided for non-commercial use. If your requirements are beyond our intended use cases, please contact us regarding your interests. The COMTOR service is monitored for its use and if necessary we will curtail non-educational access and initiate a usage discussion.

Who sponsors COMTOR?

COMTOR's development has been funded by The College of New Jersey's (TCNJ) Information Technology mini grants program, the CRA's CREU program, and the National Science Foundation.

Where is COMTOR running?

Our system for local research experimentation is hosted on the TCNJ campus. This cloud-based version is running on Amazon Web Services.

What information do you collect?

When you use the COMTOR system (this cloud-based interface), we store:

  • your email address - so that we may later determine how many times you possibly used our system
  • the date and time of your upload - so that we may later determine how frequently you used our system
  • your IP address - so that we may later determine where in the world you used our system
  • the URL of your report - so that we can debug your reports, if needed analyze the system responsiveness, and anonymously aggregate our analysis
We do not store your source code. Don't believe us? You can see exactly where we execute the call to the Amazon SDB service to store our reports and what we store. Really. Look for the method named storeCloudUse.

Will you sell the data you collect?

No.

Will you release the data you collect?

We will only release the anonymized, aggregated usage statistics in our research papers and to reports to our sponsors, like the National Science Foundation.

Where can I find more information about this project?

The formal home for the COMTOR project can be found at www.comtor.org.

I have questions. Whom should I email?

You can reach the COMTOR Principal Investigator (PI) Pete DePasquale at comtor@tcnj.edu. He can be found on Google+.

Who else is working on COMTOR?

There are four principals involved in the project at this time: Pete DePasquale, Dr. Miroslav Martinovic (CO-PI, Computer Science, TCNJ), Dr. Michael Locasto (consultant, Computer Science Department, University of Calgary), and Dr. Lisa Kaczmarczyk (Independent Assessment Consultant). A bevy of TCNJ students have additionally contributed to the overall COMTOR project.

This is cool. What else are you up to?

We have plans for additional tools and capabilities. While we do talk about some of our direction on our main web site and wiki, we are not yet ready to announce anything else at this time.

I would like to get involved. How can I do so?

Our faculty community is centering around our Google Plus presence. Developers can get involved by talking with the PI (see above) and/or visiting our SourceForge.net presence.
